You’ll have probably tired of hearing at this point that to move forward in a science or science-adjactent career, it’s all about meeting people and exploring opportunities that are presented. One way to ensure that you get a chance to explore new horizons and maximize the opportunities that come your way is to practice reaching out to people in field you find interesting.

For this level, you are going to write an email to someone that you have met briefly (or are interested in meeting) in Woods Hole. This could include:

  • Someone you’ve read papers from who is in your field
  • Someone who has given a talk in the PEP class
  • Someone who has given a talk in your department or other space in Woods Hole
  • Someone in a field in Woods Hole that you think sounds interesting
  • Anyone else that you think is interesting

We will discuss in class how you will go about this email, but some general pointers we will look at include:

  • Tell them clearly who you are, where you’re at in your science journey, and what your interests are
  • Do your research - if it’s someone who you’ve read papers from then be sure to note down what you thought was novel/interesting in their work. Also note down any questions you have about their results.
  • Be clear about what you are asking for - is it a chance to find out more about their work? Is it some specific answers to questions you are posing? Is it to have them comment on something you’re producing?
  • Be honest and genuine in your enthusiasm for what they do and relate it to your past work.

You should write your email and then bcc me so that I can see who you wrote to and what you said so I can offer feedback.
