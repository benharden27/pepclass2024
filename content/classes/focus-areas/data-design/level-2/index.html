Now that we have a little theory behind us, we’re going to look at some of the tools in R that allow us to make figures that will look really good on our poster and other publications.

Again, RStudio has a nice primer for how to customize your plots including how to zoom in, add labels, add a theme, and scale your color axis.

You should go through this primer and run the code chunks as you go to make sure you understand it.

Exercise

Once you’ve familiarized yourself with the information in the primer above, you should apply these tools to one of your visualizations.

Pick a figure you have made in R and improve it by showing me at least two design tools in actions. This could be:

  • Adding a theme
  • Adding labels or annotations
  • Zooming in
  • Changing colors
  • Adapting the theme
  • Changing tick locations and labels

Once you have done this, you should submit the following to the assignment below:

  • Your script
  • Your figure (can take screen shot or use ggsave() function)
  • A brief description of the changes you made and why you think this improves the design of your figure in relation to what you read in level 1.
